Media That Explores the Liminal

Liminal media captures the unsettling threshold between worlds — spaces that feel suspended between what is familiar and what is not. In Barton Fink, the decaying hotel corridors stretch endlessly, trapping its protagonist in creative and psychological isolation. Vivarium transforms a suburban neighborhood into a sterile labyrinth with no exit. The Truman Show and The Twilight Zone both fracture reality itself, revealing environments that feel constructed, artificial, and eerily controlled. Even The Substance and Pluribus explore bodies and identities caught in states of transition. These works linger in hallways, empty streets, television sets, and false skies — spaces that feel hollow, suspended, and quietly wrong. Liminal spaces are difficult to define, but unmistakable when encountered.
