Media That Explores the Liminal

Liminal media captures the unsettling threshold between worlds — spaces that feel suspended between what is familiar and what is not. In Barton Fink , the decaying hotel corridors stretch endlessly, trapping its protagonist in creative and psychological isolation, while Vivarium transforms a suburban neighborhood into a sterile labyrinth with no exit. The Truman Show and The Twilight Zone fracture reality itself, revealing environments that feel constructed, artificial, and eerily controlled, and works like The Substance and Pluribus explore bodies and identities caught in states of transition. More recently, the emerging Backrooms film extends this fascination with in-between spaces, bringing internet-born liminality — endless fluorescent corridors that feel both ordinary and unreal — into cinematic form. Across these works, hallways, empty streets, television sets, and false skies become suspended environments that feel quietly wrong. Liminal spaces are difficult to define, but unmistakable when encountered.
